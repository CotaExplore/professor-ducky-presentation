Professor Ducky

About this project

Professor Ducky is a Custom GPT built for the Arden University Custom GPT Challenge, exploring how AI can support student learning without replacing the thinking process.

It draws on the rubber duck debugging principle: the act of explaining your reasoning out loud to a patient, non-judgemental listener often reveals where your thinking breaks down, without anyone needing to hand you the answer. The project was also built with neurodivergent learners in mind, particularly students with ADHD, for whom the fear of judgement and the unpredictability of scheduled support can be significant barriers.

The Custom GPT uses a layered instruction architecture, four adaptive teaching modes, academic integrity safeguards, and a pastoral escalation system. The submission also includes a standalone demonstration application with a 3D animated duck, voice conversation, and a Blackboard feature for diagrams and traces.
